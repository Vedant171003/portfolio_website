Key Projects

Fireproof wall climbing rescue drone with human alive detection system
Working on a project to develop a fireproof rescue drone with an alive detection system and a wall climbing mechanism. The drone would be made of Nomex material, which will allow it to withstand high temperatures and fly through fires. It will be also equipped with a camera and an alive detection algorithm, which allows it to locate survivors in burning buildings. The wall climbing mechanism will allow the drone to climb walls and avoid obstacles. The wall climbing mechanism reference is taken from the research paper with Doi 10.1109/ACCESS.2018.2889686. The drone CAD Model is been designed in SolidWorks and its frame FEA and CFD analysis has been done in SimScale now currently we are working on its simulation inROS gazebo for wall climbing
E yantra robotics:Holonomic drive robot
In this competition we took part in a team of three and it involved implementing a simple controller to navigate three different holonomic drive simulated robots to a series of desired poses using localization in Gazebo (ROS) simulator. The challenge was to make the localization and control of the holonomic drive more realistic than in previous tasks. To achieve this, we utilized image processing techniques with OpenCV to localize the robots using an overhead camera and Aruco markers. Additionally, we implemented inverse kinematics to control the robot’s movement more accurately. By integrating these components and addressing the complexities of localization and control, we demonstrated proficiency in robotics, image processing, and algorithm development. This experience has enhanced my skills in robotics and provided valuable insights into real-world applications of autonomous systems. 
E-Baja Electric ATV (All- Terrain Vehicle)
It is a yearlong project in which we have manufactured an electric ATV (All-terrain vehicle) and participated in a competition organized by SAE E-Baja. Our project consisted of five key departments: suspension, steering, brakes, powertrain, and roll cage. I managed to design the suspension and steering geometry for our ATV in SolidWorks also managed to assemble the damper and the A- arm and H-arms for our suspension system which were manufactured by us in the workshop also assembled the braking system in our ATV.
ROS2 Multirobot Task Allocation: Algorithmic Approaches 
Embark on a journey to enhance multirobot coordination in ROS2 by investigating task allocation algorithms like Tessi, Genetic, RRT, and A*. Tessi facilitates decentralized task assignment, Genetic optimizes solutions, while RRT and A* ensure efficient path planning. Through rigorous experimentation, evaluate algorithmic performance and scalability in diverse environments. Discover how these approaches revolutionize multirobot systems by balancing task distribution and optimizing resource utilization. Gain insights into the strengths and limitations of each algorithm, paving the way for robust and adaptive task allocation strategies in complex real-world scenarios.
